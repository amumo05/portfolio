Stock Trend Analysis (2020–2025)

Tools: Python, Data Analysis, Matplotlib, CSV Processing

Overview

This project analyzes stock market trends from 2020 to 2025, a period marked by significant economic disruption and recovery. The goal was to study how different companies responded to market volatility and external events by combining numerical analysis with real-world market context.

Visuals

Detailed Explanation

The analysis uses stock data collected across multiple years and companies, focusing on key indicators such as average price change, earnings growth, and overall growth scores. Each company’s yearly performance is evaluated independently and then compared across the same time periods.

Beyond numerical trends, this project incorporates qualitative market context such as post-pandemic recovery, investor sentiment, and macroeconomic conditions. This approach allowed me to interpret not only what the trends were, but also why certain patterns emerged.

The resulting visualizations highlight long-term trajectories, periods of volatility, and cross-company differences, providing a clearer understanding of how external factors influence market performance.

This project significantly improved my ability to work with time-series data and compare trends across multiple dimensions. I learned how to design metrics that balance different indicators and how to interpret results within a broader economic context.
